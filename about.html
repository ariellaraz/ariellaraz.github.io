About

I am a PhD researcher in mathematical biology. I joined the PhD programme at the University of Sussex in October 2025 after graduating with an Integrated Master’s degree in Mathematics from the University of St Andrews.

My research focuses on mathematical models of cell migration, particularly how signalling circuits within the cytoskeleton organise protrusion and persistent movement at the leading edge of migrating cells.

Since starting my PhD I have become increasingly aware of the trade-offs involved in translating real biological systems into mathematical models. One cannot include everything, so much of the work lies in deciding which mechanisms are essential and which details can reasonably be simplified. I am particularly interested in identifying simple mathematical structures that capture the key behaviour of complex biological systems.

More broadly, I enjoy pattern recognition and data analysis, and I am interested in how mathematical structure can be used to extract insight from complex biological data.

Outside of research I enjoy yoga, chess, swimming, painting, and a good comedy show.
